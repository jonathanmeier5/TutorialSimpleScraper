<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization">Maya civilization</a></b> was a <a href="/wiki/Mesoamerica" title="Mesoamerica">Mesoamerican</a> <a href="/wiki/Civilization" title="Civilization">civilization</a> in an area that encompasses southeastern <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, all of <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a>, and the western portions of <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a> and <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>. The earliest villages of the <a href="/wiki/Maya_peoples" title="Maya peoples">Maya peoples</a> developed before 2000 BC. The first <a href="/wiki/Maya_city" title="Maya city">Maya cities</a> developed around 750 BC, and by 500 BC these cities possessed monumental <a href="/wiki/Maya_architecture" title="Maya architecture">architecture</a>. Two hundred years later, the Maya were using <a href="/wiki/Maya_script" title="Maya script">hieroglyphic writing</a>, the most advanced script in the pre-Columbian Americas. Only three of their books of history and ritual knowledge are known for certain to remain. Beginning in the mid third century, the Classic period saw the Maya civilization develop a large number of city-states linked by a complex <a href="/wiki/Trade_in_Maya_civilization" title="Trade in Maya civilization">trade network</a>. In the 9th century, there was a widespread <a href="/wiki/Classic_Maya_collapse" title="Classic Maya collapse">political collapse</a> in the central Maya region. In the 16th century, the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a> <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_the_Maya" title="Spanish conquest of the Maya">colonised the region</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Nojpet%C3%A9n" title="Nojpetén">last Maya city</a> fell in 1697. The Maya developed highly sophisticated <a href="/wiki/Maya_art" title="Maya art" class="mw-redirect">artforms</a>, complex <a href="/wiki/Maya_calendar" title="Maya calendar">calendars</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Maya_numerals" title="Maya numerals">mathematics</a> that included one of the earliest instances of the <a href="/wiki/0_(number)" title="0 (number)">explicit zero</a> in the world. <a href="/wiki/Maya_architecture" title="Maya architecture">Maya architecture</a> included palaces, <a href="/wiki/Mesoamerican_pyramids" title="Mesoamerican pyramids">pyramid-temples</a>, and ceremonial <a href="/wiki/Mesoamerican_ballcourt" title="Mesoamerican ballcourt">ballcourts</a>. (<a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization"><b>Full&#160;article...</b></a>)</p>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1142" title="1142">1142</a> – Despite having saved the <a href="/wiki/Song_dynasty" title="Song dynasty">Southern Song dynasty</a> from attempts by the northern <a href="/wiki/Jin_dynasty_(1115%E2%80%931234)" title="Jin dynasty (1115–1234)">Jin dynasty</a> to conquer it, Chinese general <b><a href="/wiki/Yue_Fei" title="Yue Fei">Yue Fei</a></b> was executed by the Song government.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1785" title="1785">1785</a> – The <b><a href="/wiki/University_of_Georgia" title="University of Georgia">University of Georgia</a></b> in <a href="/wiki/Athens,_Georgia" title="Athens, Georgia">Athens, Georgia</a>, one of the <a href="/wiki/Oldest_public_university_in_the_United_States" title="Oldest public university in the United States">oldest public universities in the United States</a>, was founded.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1868" title="1868">1868</a> – <a href="/wiki/Boshin_War" title="Boshin War">Boshin War</a>: The <b><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Toba%E2%80%93Fushimi" title="Battle of Toba–Fushimi">Battle of Toba–Fushimi</a></b>, where pro-<a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">Imperial</a> forces defeated those of the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a> and which was a catalyst for the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>, began in <a href="/wiki/Fushimi-ku,_Kyoto" title="Fushimi-ku, Kyoto">Fushimi-ku, Kyoto</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1974" title="1974">1974</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Brisbane_River" title="Brisbane River">Brisbane River</a>, which runs through the heart of <a href="/wiki/Brisbane" title="Brisbane">Brisbane</a>, broke its banks and <b><a href="/wiki/1974_Brisbane_flood" title="1974 Brisbane flood">flooded the surrounding areas</a></b> <i>(pictured)</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a> – <b><a href="/wiki/Mahamane_Ousmane" title="Mahamane Ousmane">Mahamane Ousmane</a></b>, the first democratically elected <a href="/wiki/List_of_heads_of_state_of_Niger" title="List of heads of state of Niger">president of Niger</a>, was deposed by Colonel <a href="/wiki/Ibrahim_Bar%C3%A9_Ma%C3%AFnassara" title="Ibrahim Baré Maïnassara">Ibrahim Baré Maïnassara</a> in a military <i>coup d'état</i>.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="mp-tfp-h2" style="margin:3px; background:#ddcef2; font-family:inherit; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #afa3bf; text-align:left; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em"><span class="mw-headline" id="Today.27s_featured_picture">Today's featured picture</span></h2>

<td style="padding:0 0.9em 0 0;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rows_of_bodies_of_dead_inmates_fill_the_yard_of_Lager_Nordhausen,_a_Gestapo_concentration_camp.jpg" class="image" title="The Holocaust"><img alt="The Holocaust" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Rows_of_bodies_of_dead_inmates_fill_the_yard_of_Lager_Nordhausen%2C_a_Gestapo_concentration_camp.jpg/380px-Rows_of_bodies_of_dead_inmates_fill_the_yard_of_Lager_Nordhausen%2C_a_Gestapo_concentration_camp.jpg" width="380" height="297" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Rows_of_bodies_of_dead_inmates_fill_the_yard_of_Lager_Nordhausen%2C_a_Gestapo_concentration_camp.jpg/570px-Rows_of_bodies_of_dead_inmates_fill_the_yard_of_Lager_Nordhausen%2C_a_Gestapo_concentration_camp.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Rows_of_bodies_of_dead_inmates_fill_the_yard_of_Lager_Nordhausen%2C_a_Gestapo_concentration_camp.jpg/760px-Rows_of_bodies_of_dead_inmates_fill_the_yard_of_Lager_Nordhausen%2C_a_Gestapo_concentration_camp.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="2341" /></a></td>
<td style="padding:0 6px 0 0">
<p>During <b><a href="/wiki/The_Holocaust" title="The Holocaust">The Holocaust</a></b>, approximately six million <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a> were killed by <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi regime</a> and its collaborators. Other victims of Nazi crimes included <a href="/wiki/Porajmos" title="Porajmos">Romanis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nazi_crimes_against_the_Polish_nation" title="Nazi crimes against the Polish nation">ethnic Poles</a> and other Slavs, <a href="/wiki/German_mistreatment_of_Soviet_prisoners_of_war" title="German mistreatment of Soviet prisoners of war">Soviet POWs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Communists" title="Communists" class="mw-redirect">communists</a>, <a href="/wiki/Persecution_of_homosexuals_in_Nazi_Germany_and_the_Holocaust" title="Persecution of homosexuals in Nazi Germany and the Holocaust">homosexuals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Persecution_of_Jehovah%27s_Witnesses_in_Nazi_Germany" title="Persecution of Jehovah's Witnesses in Nazi Germany">Jehovah's Witnesses</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Action_T4" title="Action T4">mentally and physically disabled</a>.</p>
<p>This photograph shows fewer than half of the bodies of the several hundred inmates who died of starvation or were shot by the <a href="/wiki/Gestapo" title="Gestapo">Gestapo</a> in the yard of the Boelcke Barracks, a subcamp of the <a href="/wiki/Mittelbau-Dora" title="Mittelbau-Dora">Mittelbau-Dora</a> <a href="/wiki/Nazi_concentration_camps" title="Nazi concentration camps">Nazi concentration camp</a> located in the south-east of the town of <a href="/wiki/Nordhausen" title="Nordhausen">Nordhausen</a>. Numbers at the camp, which was used for sick and dying inmates from January 1945, rose from a few hundred to more than six thousand by the end of the war; up to a hundred inmates died every day.</p>
